TO RABAT - ICPS 2026
Experience a Fusion of Tradition and Innovation at the 40th International Conference of Physics Students (ICPS) in Rabat, Morocco — a Historic First in Africa.

In a region where centuries-old traditions harmoniously intertwine with cutting-edge scientific advancements, a new chapter in the global physics community is about to unfold. For the very first time in African history, the prestigious 40th International Conference of Physics Students will take place in Rabat, Morocco, in 2026. As the flagship event of the International Association of Physics Students (IAPS), this landmark gathering promises to be an inspiring convergence of ideas, cultures, and innovations.

Organized by a passionate team of physics students under the auspices of the National Committee (NC) Morocco, in collaboration with the esteemed Mohamed V University, ICPS 2026 aims to elevate Africa’s presence on the global scientific stage. Rabat, uniquely positioned between the Atlantic Ocean and lush Bouregreg Valley, provides an enchanting backdrop that seamlessly marries heritage with modernity. Recognized as one of Africa’s safest and most welcoming capitals, Rabat offers international visitors a seamless blend of ancient medinas, vibrant markets, and state-of-the-art research facilities.

The scientific program will be as dynamic and diverse as the city itself. Attendees can look forward to a rich lineup of keynote speeches, interactive panels, and hands-on tutorials led by prominent Moroccan and international physicists. From the mysteries of quantum mechanics to breakthroughs in renewable energy, the sessions will highlight Morocco's rapidly growing contributions to the global scientific landscape. Participants will have unparalleled opportunities to network with leading researchers from national institutions, universities, and innovation hubs, fostering collaborations that could shape the future of physics worldwide.

But ICPS 2026 is about more than just scientific exchange — it’s a cultural celebration. The event will feature immersive experiences that showcase Morocco’s vibrant heritage: from soulful Andalusian melodies and traditional Amazigh dances to mint tea ceremonies and flavorful couscous feasts. Attendees will explore iconic sites such as the magnificent Kasbah of the Udayas, stroll through the lush gardens of the Royal Palace, and marvel at Rabat’s distinctive Art Deco architecture, all while immersing themselves in UNESCO World Heritage treasures.

Explore beyond the conference with unforgettable excursions. Surf the Atlantic waves, embark on desert adventures in Merzouga, or enjoy star-studded nights in the majestic Atlas Mountains. Don’t miss Moroccan Night — a lively celebration where you can don traditional attire like djellabas or caftans, savor local music and cuisine, and dance under the starlit sky.

With its legendary warmth, rich cultural tapestry, and a thriving academic scene, Rabat stands ready to welcome the world's brightest physics students for an ICPS experience unlike any other. Prepare for inspiring discoveries, meaningful connections, and unforgettable memories in this historic African hub of science and culture.

Venue

ICPS 2026 in Rabat, Morocco’s capital, offers a unique cultural journey where tradition meets modernity. Participants will explore the city’s vibrant medinas, historic landmarks, and coastal charm, while experiencing Moroccan hospitality, cuisine, music, and art. From guided tours of Rabat’s UNESCO World Heritage Sites to evenings filled with local flavors and performances, the week promises a rich immersion into Morocco’s diverse cultural heritage.The conference will be held at Mohammed V University, a prestigious institution located in the heart of Rabat. The venue offers state-of-the-art facilities, beautiful gardens, and a vibrant academic atmosphere, making it the perfect backdrop for this international gathering of physics students.

Travel Info

Essential details to help you prepare for ICPS 2026 in Rabat.

Getting to Rabat

Arrive via Rabat-Salé Airport (RBA), just minutes from downtown, or Casablanca’s Mohammed V Airport (CMN), about an hour by train. Shuttles and taxis available.

Visa Requirements

Many nationalities can enter Morocco visa-free for up to 90 days. Check requirements early and request an invitation letter if needed.

Accommodation

Options from conference hotels, budget hostels, to traditional riads. Book early to secure your stay.

Currency & Payments

Moroccan Dirham (MAD) is the currency. ATMs are common; cards accepted widely, but keep cash handy for small expenses.
